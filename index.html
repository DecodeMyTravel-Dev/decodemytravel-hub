3. Decoder Logic & Assumptions

  • Pricing: We are assuming $14 drinks and $8 beer as default baselines.
  • The Gratuity Factor: We add 20% to your a la carte total automatically. If you spend $100 at the bar, our tool knows your bill is actually $120. The gratuity percentage is included in the equation.
  • The Port Day Penalty: Remember: if you are on a Mediterranean or Alaska itinerary with long port hours, your "window" to drink on the ship is smaller, making packages harder to justify.
  • Included Value: For Princess, where the package includes crew appreciation (tips), the "Package" side of the math is significantly more favorable.

See your actual cruise specifics for details and current pricing. Pre-paying will usually lock in your price. Waiting until after a price increase may incur a surcharge.

Baggage Considerations: The 2026 Manual

1. The Standard for Major Carriers

American Airlines, Delta, United, and many national and International Airlines. In 2026, the "Standard" has moved to Volumetric Compliance. If your bag doesn't slide into the automated metal sizer without a shove, it's a gate-check.

  • PERSONAL ITEM: 17x10x9in. Must fit under seat. (Commonly the only item allowed for Basic Economy).
  • CARRY-ON: 22x14x9in. Includes wheels/handles.
  • CHECKED (ECONOMY): 50 lbs (23 kg). Fees average $45-$50. Small discounts often apply for pre-paying before check-in.
  • CHECKED (FIRST/BUSINESS): 70 lbs (32 kg). Premium tickets typically include 2-3 bags.

2. Regional Airlines

Traveling outside major hubs requires extreme caution regarding Weight Sensitivity. Unlike US domestic flights, international regional carriers often cap carry-ons at 15–22 lbs (7–10 kg).

The Regional Rule: Many carriers (2026) allow only 10kg for Economy and 12kg for Business. Anything over is charged per kilo at the counter.

3. Surcharges & The 2026 "Fuel Factor"

Overweight Fees: Crossing the 50 lb. mark in Economy triggers surcharges of $100–$200 for bags up to 70 lbs.

Fuel Disclaimers: Many 2026 tickets include a "Variable Fuel Surcharge" (YQ/YR codes). Some regional carriers in high-cost zones apply an additional $10–$25 "Security & Fuel Handling" fee per checked bag at the counter.

These guidelines are provided as a best estimate. Consult your carrier’s website 72 hours before flying for current limits and pricing.
