Baggage Considerations: The 2026 Manual

1. The Standard for Major Carriers

American Airlines, Delta, United, and many national and International Airlines. In 2026, the "Standard" has moved to Volumetric Compliance. If your bag doesn't slide into the automated metal sizer without a shove, it's a gate-check.

  • Personal Item: 17x10x9in. Must fit under seat. (Basic Economy passengers restricted exclusively to this).
  • Carry-On: 22x14x9in. Must include wheels and handles in measurement.
  • Checked (Economy): 50 lbs (23 kg). Fees avg $45-$50. Discounts often apply for pre-paying before check-in.
  • Checked (First/Business): 70 lbs (32 kg) limit. Most premium tickets include 2-3 bags at this weight free.

2. Regional Airlines

Traveling outside the major hubs requires extreme caution regarding weight sensitivity. Unlike US domestic flights, international regional carriers often cap carry-ons at 15–22 lbs (7–10 kg). Many carriers (2026) allow only 10kg for Economy and 12kg for Business.

3. Surcharges & The 2026 "Fuel Factor"

Overweight Fees: Crossing the 50 lb. mark in Economy is expensive. Surcharges for 51-70 lbs typically range from $100–$200.

Fuel Disclaimers: Many 2026 tickets include a "Variable Fuel Surcharge" (YQ/YR codes). Some regional carriers may apply an additional $10–$25 "Security & Fuel Handling" fee per checked bag at the counter.

These guidelines are provided as a best estimate at the time of publishing. Consult your carrier’s website a few days before flying for current limits and pricing.
