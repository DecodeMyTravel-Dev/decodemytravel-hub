3. Decoder Logic & Assumptions
To give you a best estimate breakeven point, this tool utilizes the following 2026 benchmarks:

Baggage Considerations: 2026 Manual

1. The Standard for the "Big Three" (American, Delta, United)
In 2026, the "Standard" has moved to Volumetric Compliance. If your bag doesn't slide into the automated metal sizer without a shove, it's a gate-check.

  • Personal Item: 17 x 10 x 9 inches. Must fit entirely under the seat.
  • Carry-On: 22 x 14 x 9 inches. Must include wheels and handles in your measurement.
  • Checked Bag (Economy): 50 lbs (23 kg) limit. Fees average $45–$50 for the first bag if not prepaid.
  • Checked Bag (First/Business): 70 lbs (32 kg) limit. Most premium tickets include 2-3 bags for free.

2. Regional Airlines
Weight Sensitivity: International regional carriers often cap carry-ons at 15–22 lbs (7–10 kg). If you are over, they will move it to the hold at the jet bridge.

The Regional Rule: Many Regional carriers (as of 2026) allow only 10kg for Economy and 12kg for Business for carry-ons. Anything over is charged per kilo at the counter.

3. Surcharges & The 2026 "Fuel Factor"
Overweight Fees: Crossing the 50 lb. mark in Economy is expensive ($100–$200 surcharge). Fuel Disclaimers: Some regional carriers may apply an additional $10–$25 "Security & Fuel Handling" fee per checked bag at the counter.

These guidelines are provided as a best estimate. Consult your carrier’s website for current limits and pricing.

The Global Tipping Manual (2026)

1. The "Automatic" Trap: 2026 Auto-Gratuity ($18-$20/day) covers room/dining staff, but NOT shore excursion guides.

2. Shore Excursion Etiquette: Drivers: $2-$5 per person. Specialist Guides: $10-$20. Private Drivers: 10-15% of cost.

3. Port Intelligence: Look for "Servicio Incluido" or "Propina" on your bill before adding extra. USD $1 bills are essential for small tips.
