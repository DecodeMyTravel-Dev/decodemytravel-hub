Baggage Considerations: The 2026 Manual

1. The Standard for Major Carriers

American Airlines, Delta, United, and many international flag carriers.

In 2026, the "Standard" has moved to Volumetric Compliance. If your bag doesn't slide into the automated metal sizer without a shove, it's a gate-check.

  • PERSONAL ITEM: 17 x 10 x 9 inches. Must fit entirely under the seat. Tip: Basic Economy passengers are often restricted exclusively to this item.
  • CARRY-ON: 22 x 14 x 9 inches. Must include wheels and handles in your measurement.
  • CHECKED (ECONOMY): 50 lbs (23 kg) limit. Fees now average $45–$50 for the first bag if not prepaid. A small discount may be applied when purchasing before flight check-in.
  • CHECKED (FIRST/BUSINESS): 70 lbs (32 kg) limit. Most premium tickets include 2-3 bags at this weight for free.

2. Regional Airlines

Traveling outside the major hubs requires extreme caution.

Weight Sensitivity: Unlike US domestic flights, international regional carriers often cap carry-ons at 15–22 lbs (7–10 kg). If you are over, they will move it to the hold at the jet bridge.

The Regional Rule: Many Regional carriers (as of 2026) allow only 10kg for Economy and 12kg for Business for carry-ons. Anything over is charged per kilo at the counter. See actual fees for your specific flight.

3. Surcharges & The 2026 "Fuel Factor"

Overweight Fees: Crossing the 50 lb. mark in Economy is expensive. Most airlines now charge a flat $100–$200 surcharge for bags between 51 and 70 lbs.

Fuel Disclaimers: Many 2026 tickets now include a "Variable Fuel Surcharge" (YQ/YR codes). While this is usually in the fare, some regional carriers in high-cost zones may apply an additional $10–$25 "Security & Fuel Handling" fee per checked bag at the counter.

These guidelines are provided as a best estimate at the time of publishing. Consult your carrier’s website a few days before flying for current limits and pricing.
