Baggage Considerations: The 2026 Manual

1. Standard for Major Carriers (AA, Delta, United): Standard for 2026 is Volumetric Compliance. If it doesn't fit the sizer, it's a gate-check.

  • Personal Item: 17x10x9in. Must fit under seat. (Basic Economy restricted to this).
  • Carry-On: 22x14x9in. Must include wheels and handles.
  • Checked (Economy): 50 lbs (23 kg). Fees avg $45-$50.
  • Checked (First/Business): 70 lbs (32 kg) limit.

2. Regional Airlines: Extreme weight sensitivity. International regional carriers often cap carry-ons at 15–22 lbs (7–10 kg). Many only allow 10kg for Economy and 12kg for Business.

3. Surcharges & Fuel: Overweight fees ($100–$200) for 51-70 lbs. Many tickets include a "Variable Fuel Surcharge" (YQ/YR codes) at the counter.

Consult your carrier’s website a few days before flying for current limits.

Tipping/Gratuities Guide

1. The "Automatic" Trap: 2026 Auto-Gratuity ($18-$20/day) covers room/dining staff, but NOT shore excursion guides.

2. Currency Intelligence: USD is King in Mexico/Bahamas. In Europe, USD is a burden; use local currency for small tips.

3. Shore Excursion Etiquette: Drivers: $2-$5. Specialist Guides: $10-$20. Private Drivers: 10-15% of cost.

4. Regional Philosophy: Asia (Japan/Korea) is often zero-tip. Mediterranean is "round-up." 20% is considered clueless tourist behavior in many EU ports.
