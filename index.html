3. Decoder Logic & Assumptions

  • Pricing: We are assuming $14 drinks and $8 beer.
  • The Gratuity Factor: We add 20% to your a la carte total automatically. If you spend $100 at the bar, our tool knows your bill is actually $120.
  • The Port Day Penalty: The decoder assumes a daily average. if you are on long port hours, your "window" to drink on the ship is smaller.
  • Included Value: For Princess, where the package includes crew appreciation (tips), the "Package" side of the math is significantly more favorable.

Baggage Considerations

1. The Standard for the "Big Three", and many other national and International Airlines
American Airlines, Delta Airlines, United Airlines. In 2026, the "Standard" has moved to Volumetric Compliance. If your bag doesn't slide into the automated metal sizer without a shove, it's a gate-check.

  • Personal Item: 17 x 10 x 9 inches. Must fit entirely under the seat. Tip: Basic Economy passengers are often restricted exclusively to this item.
  • Carry-On: 22 x 14 x 9 inches. Must include wheels and handles in your measurement.
  • Checked Bag (Economy): 50 lbs (23 kg) limit. Fees now average $45–$50 for the first bag if not prepaid.
  • Checked Bag (First/Business): 70 lbs (32 kg) limit. Most premium tickets include 2-3 bags at this weight for free.

2. Regional Airlines
Traveling outside the major hubs requires extreme caution.

  • Weight Sensitivity: international regional carriers often cap carry-ons at 15–22 lbs (7–10 kg). If you are over, they will move it to the hold.
  • The Regional Rule: Many Regional carriers (as of 2026) allow only 10kg for Economy and 12kg for Business for carry-ons. Anything over is charged per kilo at the counter.

3. Surcharges & The 2026 "Fuel Factor"

  • Overweight Fees: Crossing the 50 lb. mark in Economy is expensive. Most airlines now charge a flat $100–$200 surcharge for bags between 51 and 70 lbs.
  • Fuel Disclaimers: Many 2026 tickets now include a "Variable Fuel Surcharge" (YQ/YR codes). Some regional carriers may apply an additional $10–$25 "Security & Fuel Handling" fee per checked bag at the counter.

Provided as a best estimate. Consult your carrier’s website for current limits and pricing.

The Global Tipping Manual (2026)

1. The "Automatic" Trap: 2026 Auto-Gratuity ($18-$20/day) covers room/dining staff, but NOT shore excursion guides.

2. Shore Excursion Etiquette: Drivers: $2-$5 per person. Specialist Guides: $10-$20. Private Drivers: 10-15% of cost.

3. Port Intelligence: Look for "Servicio Incluido" or "Propina" on your bill before adding extra. USD $1 bills are essential for small tips in the Caribbean and Bahamas.
