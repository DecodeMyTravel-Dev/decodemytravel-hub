Baggage Considerations: The 2026 Manual

1. The Standard for the "Big Three", and many other national and International Airlines
American Airlines, Delta Airlines, United Airlines. In 2026, the "Standard" has moved to Volumetric Compliance. If your bag doesn't slide into the automated metal sizer without a shove, it's a gate-check.

  • Personal Item: 17 x 10 x 9 inches. Must fit entirely under the seat. (Tip: Basic Economy passengers are often restricted exclusively to this item).
  • Carry-On: 22 x 14 x 9 inches. Must include wheels and handles in your measurement.
  • Checked Bag (Economy): 50 lbs (23 kg) limit. Fees now average $45–$50 for the first bag if not prepaid. A small discount may apply when purchasing before flight check-in.
  • Checked Bag (First/Business): 70 lbs (32 kg) limit. Most premium tickets include 2-3 bags at this weight free.

2. Regional Airlines
Traveling outside the major hubs requires extreme caution. International regional carriers often cap carry-ons at 15–22 lbs (7–10 kg). Many carriers (2026) allow only 10kg for Economy and 12kg for Business.

3. Surcharges & The 2026 "Fuel Factor"
Overweight Fees: Crossing the 50 lb. mark in Economy is expensive. Surcharges typically range from $100–$200.
Fuel Disclaimers: Many 2026 tickets include a "Variable Fuel Surcharge" (YQ/YR codes). Some regional carriers may apply an additional $10–$25 "Security & Fuel Handling" fee per checked bag at the counter.

The Global Tipping Manual (2026)

1. The "Automatic" Trap
In 2026, most cruise lines and many port-city restaurants have moved to "Auto-Gratuity."

  • Cruises: The $18-$20/day "Daily Appreciation" covers the room steward and dining staff, but does not cover shore excursion guides.
  • Port Restaurants: Look for the words "Servicio Incluido" or "Propina" on the receipt before adding a single dollar.

2. Currency Intelligence
USD is "King" in Mexico and the Bahamas. However, in Europe, USD is a burden for locals. The "One Dollar" Strategy: Carrying a stack of crisp $1 bills is still the most effective tool for a cruiser in the Western Hemisphere.

3. Shore Excursion Etiquette (2026 Rates)

  • Bus Driver: $2 - $5 per person.
  • Specialist Guide (Snorkel/Zipline/Historian): $10 - $20 per person.
  • Private Drivers: 10-15% of the total tour cost.

4. Regional Philosophy
Mediterranean "Round-Up": 20% in Italy makes you look like a "clueless tourist." Asia "Zero-Tip" Rule: Leaving money on a table in Japan is considered "forgotten cash" rather than a reward.
